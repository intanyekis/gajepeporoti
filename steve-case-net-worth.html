Early Life and Education

Steve Case, a renowned entrepreneur and co-founder of America Online (AOL), had a fascinating early life and educational journey that shaped his path to success. Born on August 21, 1958, in Honolulu, Hawaii, Case grew up with a passion for technology and innovation.

He attended Punahou School, a prestigious institution that nurtured his academic and personal development. Later, Case pursued higher education at Williams College, where he graduated with a degree in political science. This diverse educational background equipped him with a unique perspective that would prove invaluable in his entrepreneurial endeavors.

After completing his studies, Case embarked on his professional journey, initially working at Procter & Gamble. However, his true passion lay in the rapidly evolving tech landscape. He transitioned into the marketing department at Pizza Hut, where he gained valuable experience in the business world.

Case’s passion for technology led him to Control Video Corporation (CVC), a company that paved the way for his future success. Joining CVC marked a pivotal moment in his career, as he became a driving force behind the development of online services and interactive games.

“Education and early experiences played a vital role in Steve Case’s journey. They provided him with the foundation and skills necessary to thrive in the dynamic world of technology.”

Through his early life and education, Steve Case demonstrated a natural inclination towards innovation and entrepreneurship. These formative experiences laid the groundwork for his future successes and set him on a path to revolutionize the technology industry.

Career Beginnings

In the early 1980s, Steve Case embarked on his professional journey with Control Video Corporation (CVC). Starting as the vice president of marketing, Case quickly established himself as a visionary and innovative thinker in the nascent digital landscape.

At CVC, Case played a pivotal role in the development of online services and interactive games, foreseeing the potential of the emerging technologies. His strategic mindset and passion for creating engaging user experiences propelled him to the forefront of the technology industry.

“The future of the digital era was taking shape, and I wanted to be at the forefront of this revolution,” Case reminisces.

Under Case’s leadership, Control Video Corporation transformed into the iconic America Online (AOL). As CEO of AOL, Case spearheaded the company’s meteoric rise, revolutionizing the way people accessed and interacted with the internet.

With his unparalleled expertise and relentless drive, Steve Case laid the foundation for his remarkable career and cemented his status as a pioneer in the digital realm.

Revolution

After leaving AOL, Steve Case embarked on a new venture by founding Revolution LLC, an investment firm dedicated to supporting entrepreneurs and disruptive companies. Revolution has three major funds: Revolution Ventures, Revolution Growth, and Revolution Places. Through these funds, Case has made strategic investments in a wide range of companies, including Zipcar and Sweetgreen.

Revolution Ventures focuses on early-stage technology companies, providing them with the necessary capital and guidance to drive innovation and growth.

Revolution Growth, on the other hand, targets companies that have already reached a certain level of success and are ready for the next phase of expansion.

Revolution Places is a fund dedicated to investing in real estate and hospitality projects that aim to revitalize communities and create long-term value.

Through Revolution LLC’s various funds, Steve Case has been able to support and nurture groundbreaking ideas and businesses, fueling technological advancements and shaping the future of industries. His commitment to fostering innovation and driving change has solidified his position as a leading figure in the investment world.

Philanthropy and Advocacy

Steve Case and his wife Jean are passionate about philanthropy and are committed to making a positive impact on society. Through the Case Foundation, they actively support initiatives that foster entrepreneurship, social impact, and technology innovation. Their philanthropic efforts aim to create a more inclusive and equitable society, where everyone has the opportunity to succeed.

One of the key initiatives supported by the Case Foundation is the Startup America Partnership, a national campaign that aims to accelerate high-growth entrepreneurship and job creation. As part of this partnership, the Case Foundation provides resources, mentorship, and funding to aspiring entrepreneurs, helping them turn their ideas into successful businesses.

Steve Case also serves as a co-chair of the Bipartisan Policy Center’s Democracy Project. In this role, he advocates for policies that promote innovation, economic growth, and job creation. One area of focus for Case is immigration reform, which he believes is crucial for supporting startup growth and innovation in the United States.

“We need to ensure that talented entrepreneurs from around the world have the opportunity to contribute to our economy and create jobs. Immigration reform is not just an economic imperative, but also a critical step towards fostering innovation and driving technological progress.”

Steve Case’s advocacy efforts in support of immigration reform align with his belief that diverse perspectives and talent are essential for driving innovation and building successful businesses.

Books

Steve Case is not only a successful entrepreneur and investor but also a published author. He has written two influential books that offer valuable insights into the world of technology and entrepreneurship.

“The Third Wave: An Entrepreneur’s Vision of the Future” takes readers on a journey through the past, present, and future of the internet. Case shares his vision of the “Third Wave” of the internet, where entrepreneurs will need to collaborate with established companies and work closely with governments to navigate the challenges and opportunities of a digitally connected world. This thought-provoking book provides a roadmap for those seeking to capitalize on emerging technologies and disrupt traditional industries.

“The Rise of the Rest: How Entrepreneurs in Surprising Places are Building the New American Dream” explores the rise of entrepreneurship in unexpected locations across the United States, outside of traditional tech hubs like Silicon Valley. In this book, Case highlights the untapped potential of these “rise of the rest” cities and shares inspiring stories of entrepreneurs who are building innovative businesses and revitalizing local economies. Case’s firsthand experiences and insights provide a fresh perspective on the changing landscape of American entrepreneurship.

“The Third Wave: An Entrepreneur’s Vision of the Future” and “The Rise of the Rest: How Entrepreneurs in Surprising Places are Building the New American Dream” are must-reads for aspiring entrepreneurs, established business leaders, and anyone interested in understanding the future of technology, entrepreneurship, and the power of partnerships between startups and established corporations.

When it comes to real estate, Steve Case’s investments extend far beyond the mainland. He is known for owning tens of thousands of acres of land in the beautiful state of Hawaii. One of his notable acquisitions includes a controlling interest in Grove Farm, a historic Hawaiian company with a rich legacy in agriculture and land development.

Grove Farm, founded in 1864, is located on the island of Kauai and spans over 30,000 acres of land. The company is involved in various real estate ventures, including commercial, residential, and agricultural properties. Steve Case’s ownership of Grove Farm positions him as a significant player in Hawaii’s real estate market.

In addition to Grove Farm, Case also holds a significant interest in Maui Land & Pineapple Company.
